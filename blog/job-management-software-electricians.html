The Best Job Management Software for UK Electricians

By Jamie, a working spark · 9 min read

The right job management software turns the worst part of the job, the quoting and invoicing and chasing, into a few taps on your phone. The wrong one is another monthly bill you never log into. Here is the honest rundown for 2026.

Tradify — best all-rounder for sole traders

Clean, simple, syncs with Xero, fast to set up. Quoting, scheduling, job tracking and invoicing in one app. Most sparks pay somewhere around £34 to £68 a month depending on team size. If you want one tool that just works, start here.

Powered Now — best for EICRs and UK compliance

The only proper UK-native option for the compliance stuff. Built-in EICR and Minor Works certificates, nearly 100 electrical forms, digital signatures on site, and it handles CIS and the VAT reverse charge properly. If you live in certificates, this is your tool.

ServiceM8 — best for callouts and Apple users

Charges per job rather than per user, which suits high-volume, lower-value work like emergency callouts and fault finding. Slick on iPhone and iPad. We set this one up for clients a lot and wire it into the rest of their systems.

Jobber — best for bigger teams

If there are five or more of you, Jobber's scheduling, dispatch and client portal earn their keep. Customers approve quotes and pay invoices online without ringing the office.

The bit nobody tells you

Software only saves you time if it is set up right and talking to the rest of your business. A job tool that is not connected to your website, your leads and your calendar just means typing everything twice. That is the bit we sort: pick the right tool, set it up properly, and plumb it into your website and lead system so a job flows from enquiry to invoice without you retyping a thing.
