How to Get Electrician Leads Without Checkatrade

By Jamie, a working spark · 9 min read

How much did you hand Checkatrade last year? Have a proper think. A ton a month, plus a fee every time you want a lead. A lead they have also flogged to three other sparks down the road.

That is the bit that grates. You are not buying work, you are renting a chance at it, and so is everyone else. Here is how to get leads that are actually yours.

Why Checkatrade and MyBuilder Keep You Stuck

The same enquiry gets sold to several tradesmen at once, so you are straight into a race to the bottom on price. You own nothing. And the minute you stop paying, the leads stop dead. It is rented work, not a business.

There is also no loyalty to you in any of it. Checkatrade does not care whether you win the job or not. They get paid either way. And the better you do on their platform, the more they charge you to stay visible on it.

1. Get Found on Google Without a Middleman

Set up your Google Business Profile with your services, service areas and real photos of your work, then link it to a proper electrician website. When someone in your area searches "electrician near me", you want to be one of the three names on that map. Nobody else is getting that same enquiry. You own that lead, and the next one, and the one after that. See the full picture in our electrician marketing guide.

2. Stop Losing Missed Calls to Another Spark

Missed call text back fires an automatic message the second you cannot pick up: "Sorry I missed you, I will call back shortly. What did you need doing?" Most folk will not leave a voicemail, they just ring the next spark. With the text, most of them wait. You win back a job or two a week without lifting a finger. We set this up as part of the system so you never have to think about it.

3. Build Your Own Google Review Engine

Reviews are free, they compound, and they win the job over the bloke with four. The sparks at the top of Google Maps are almost always the ones with the most and newest reviews. Ask every happy customer the same day, while you are still fresh in their mind, and send them a direct link so they do not have to go hunting. Full step by step system in our guide on how to get more Google reviews as an electrician.

4. Tap the Work Already Sitting in Your Customer List

Your past customers are the cheapest work there is. A quick text saying "Hi, it's [your name], we sorted your consumer unit last year. Just letting you know we have some space coming up if you have anything that needs doing" will get you a job or two every time. And ask for referrals on every good job, do not just hope for them. If you want to make this automatic, see the best job management software for electricians.

5. Work Out What Checkatrade Is Actually Costing You

Add up a year of Checkatrade fees plus per-lead costs. Many sparks are spending £150 to £300 a month or more, and still competing for shared leads. That is £1,800 to £3,600 a year going out the door for work you are racing three other sparks to win. Now imagine that same money going into systems you own that bring leads in on their own, forever.
